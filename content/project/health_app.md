<div class="banner-title">Health Survey Application</div>

I collaborated extensively with interdisciplinary researchers on the design and
analysis of longitudinal and cross-sectional health surveys at local, national, 
and international levels.

<div style="height: 20px;"></div>

<div class="project-block">
  <div class="project-text">
§ denote trainees.

<p style="color: #2f6db5; font-weight: 700; font-size: 1.2rem; margin-bottom: 0.5rem;">
The University of Dioxin Exposure Study (UMDES),
</p>
begun in 2004, was 
undertaken in response to concerns that the discharge of dioxin-like compounds 
from the Dow Chemical Company facilities in Midland, Michigan, resulted in 
contamination of soils in the Tittabawassee River floodplain and areas of the 
city of Midland, Michigan, leading to an increase in residents’ body burdens 
of polychlorinated dibenzodioxins and polychlorinated dibenzofurans. The sample
design was a stratified, multistage area probability sample of households and 
persons.

  </div>
</div>

**Selected publications:**

- Garabrant, D., Franzblau, A., Lepkowski, J., Gillespie, B., Adriaens, P., Demond, A., Ward, B., LaDronka, K., Hedgeman, E., Knutson, K., Zwica, L., Olson, K., Towey, T., **Chen, Q.**, and Hong, B. (2009). “The University of Michigan Dioxin Exposure Study: methods for an environmental exposure study of polychlorinated dioxins, furans and biphenyls”, *Environmental Health Perspectives*, **117**, 803–810.

- Hedgeman, E., **Chen, Q.**, Hong, B., Chang, C.-W., Olson, K., LaDronka, K., Ward, B., Adriaens, P., Demond, A., Gillespie, B., Lepkowski, J., Franzblau, A., and Garabrant, D. (2009). “The University of Michigan Dioxin Exposure Study: population survey results and serum concentrations for polychlorinated dioxins, furans and biphenyls”, *Environmental Health Perspectives*, **117**, 811–817.

<div style="height: 20px;"></div>

<div class="project-block">
  <div class="project-text">

<p style="color: #2f6db5; font-weight: 700; font-size: 1.2rem; margin-bottom: 0.5rem;">
The Ohio Army National Guard Mental Health Initiative (OHARNG-MHI),
</p>
begun in 2008, is a longitudinal research study evaluating risk factors, resilience, and
mental health outcomes like PTSD, depression, and anxiety among National Guard 
soldiers before, during, and after deployments in the Ohio Army National Guard. 

  </div>
</div>

**Selected publications:**

- Fink, D. S., **Chen, Q.**, Liu, Y., Tamburrino, M. B., Galea, S., Liberzon, I., Shirley, E., Fine, T., Cohen, G. H., and Calabrese, J. R. (2016). “Incidence and risk for mood and anxiety disorders in a representative sample of Ohio Army National Guard members”, *Public Health Reports*, **131**, 614–622.

- Anthopolos, R., **Chen, Q.**, Sedransk, J., Thompson, M., Meng, G., and Galea, S. (2023). “A Bayesian growth mixture model for complex survey data: clustering post-disaster PTSD trajectories”, *Annals of Applied Statistics*, **17**(3), 2494–2514.

<div style="height: 20px;"></div>

<div class="project-block">
  <div class="project-text">
  
<p style="color: #2f6db5; font-weight: 700; font-size: 1.2rem; margin-bottom: 0.5rem;">
The 2013-2014 National Drug Abuse Treatment System Survey (NDATSS-2013)
</p>
provides management- and policy-relevant information about substance abuse 
treatment in the United States. Substance abuse treatment programs are chosen 
for the study by random selection from a list of all such programs stratified by
program types, provided by the Substance Abuse and Mental Health Services 
Administration (SAMHSA). The data collection is done through interviews with 
program directors and clinical supervisors. 

  </div>
</div>

**Selected publications:**

- D’Aunno, T., Friedmann, P. D., **Chen, Q.**, and Wilson, D. M. (2015). “Integration of substance abuse treatment organizations into Accountable Care Organizations: results from a national survey”, *Journal of Health Politics, Policy, and Law*, **40**(4), 795–817.

- D’Aunno, T., Pollack, H., **Chen, Q.**, and Friedmann, P. D. (2017). “Linkages between patient-centered medical homes and addiction treatment organizations: results from a national survey”, *Medical Care*, **55**(4), 379–383.

<div style="height: 20px;"></div>

<div class="project-block">
  <div class="project-text">

<p style="color: #2f6db5; font-weight: 700; font-size: 1.2rem; margin-bottom: 0.5rem;">
Lesotho Cell Phone PHIA (LeCellPHIA),
</p>
a cell phone surveillance system that 
collects weekly population-based data on influenza-like illness in Lesotho by 
calling a representative sample of 2020 Lesotho Population HIV Impact Assessment
(LePHIA2020) survey participants aged ≥18 years that completed the LePHIA2020 
interview, consented to follow-up, and provided a valid phone number. 
The surveillance system launched on July 1, 2020, beginning with a 2-week 
enrollment period followed by weekly calls that continued until September 30, 2022.

  </div>
</div>

**Selected publications:**

- Greenleaf, A. R., Mwima, G., Lethoko, M., Conkling, M., Keefer, G., Chang, C., McLeod, N., Maruyama, H., **Chen, Q.**, Farley, S. M., and Low, A. (2021). “Participatory Surveillance of COVID-19 in Lesotho via Weekly Calls: Protocol for Cell Phone Data Collection”, *JMIR Research Protocols*, **10**(9), e31236.

- Greenleaf, A. R., Francis, S., Zou, J., Farley, S. M., Lekhela, T., Asiimwe, F., and **Chen, Q.** (2024). “Influenza-like illness symptoms as reported by a population-based participatory surveillance system in Lesotho, July 2020–July 2021”, *JMIR Public Health and Surveillance*, **10**, e55208.

<div class="project-block">
  <div class="project-text">

<p style="color: #2f6db5; font-weight: 700; font-size: 1.2rem; margin-bottom: 0.5rem;">
Uganda Refugee Population-based HIV Impact Assessment (RUPHIA)
</p>
2021 was a 
cross-sectional, population-based HIV survey among refugee households in Ugandan
settlements, excluding Kampala. A gridded population sampling approach was used
which redistributed the population into manageable-sized areas of contiguous 
grid cells based on their estimated population size, forming enumeration area-like
sampling units using the publicly available GridEZ algorithm.

  </div>
</div>
