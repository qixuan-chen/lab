Health Survey Application

I collaborated extensively with interdisciplinary researchers on the design and analysis of longitudinal and cross-sectional health surveys at local, national, and international levels.

§ denote trainees.

The University of Dioxin Exposure Study (UMDES),

begun in 2004, was undertaken in response to concerns that the discharge of dioxin-like compounds from the Dow Chemical Company facilities in Midland, Michigan, resulted in contamination of soils in the Tittabawassee River floodplain and areas of the city of Midland, Michigan, leading to an increase in residents’ body burdens of polychlorinated dibenzodioxins and polychlorinated dibenzofurans. The sample design was a stratified, multistage area probability sample of households and persons.

The Ohio Army National Guard Mental Health Initiative (OHARNG-MHI),

begun in 2008, is a longitudinal research study evaluating risk factors, resilience, and mental health outcomes like PTSD, depression, and anxiety among National Guard soldiers before, during, and after deployments in the Ohio Army National Guard.

The 2013-2014 National Drug Abuse Treatment System Survey (NDATSS-2013)

provides management- and policy-relevant information about substance abuse treatment in the United States. Substance abuse treatment programs are chosen for the study by random selection from a list of all such programs stratified by program types, provided by the Substance Abuse and Mental Health Services Administration (SAMHSA). The data collection is done through interviews with program directors and clinical supervisors.

Selected publications:

  • D’Aunno, T., Friedmann, P. D., Chen, Q., and Wilson, D. M. (2015). “Integration of substance abuse treatment organizations into Accountable Care Organizations: results from a national survey”, Journal of Health Politics, Policy, and Law, 40(4), 795–817.

  • D’Aunno, T., Pollack, H., Chen, Q., and Friedmann, P. D. (2017). “Linkages between patient-centered medical homes and addiction treatment organizations: results from a national survey”, Medical Care, 55(4), 379–383.

Lesotho Cell Phone PHIA (LeCellPHIA),

a cell phone surveillance system that collects weekly population-based data on influenza-like illness in Lesotho by calling a representative sample of 2020 Lesotho Population HIV Impact Assessment (LePHIA2020) survey participants aged ≥18 years that completed the LePHIA2020 interview, consented to follow-up, and provided a valid phone number. The surveillance system launched on July 1, 2020, beginning with a 2-week enrollment period followed by weekly calls that continued until September 30, 2022.

Uganda Refugee Population-based HIV Impact Assessment (RUPHIA)

2021 was a cross-sectional, population-based HIV survey among refugee households in Ugandan settlements, excluding Kampala. A gridded population sampling approach was used which redistributed the population into manageable-sized areas of contiguous grid cells based on their estimated population size, forming enumeration area-like sampling units using the publicly available GridEZ algorithm.
