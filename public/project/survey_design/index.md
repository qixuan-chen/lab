<div class="banner-title">Survey Design</div>

I collaborated extensively with interdisciplinary researchers on the design 
and analysis of longitudinal and cross-sectional health surveys at local, national, 
and international levels.

<div style="height: 20px;"></div>

<div class="project-block">
  <div class="project-text">


<p style="color: #2f6db5; font-weight: 700; font-size: 1.2rem; margin-bottom: 0.5rem;">
The University of Dioxin Exposure Study (UMDES)
</p>
was undertaken in response to 
concerns that the discharge of dioxin-like compounds from the Dow Chemical Company 
facilities in Midland, Michigan, resulted in contamination of soils in the 
Tittabawassee River floodplain and areas of the city of Midland, Michigan, 
leading to an increase in residents’ body burdens of polychlorinated dibenzodioxins
and polychlorinated dibenzofurans. The sample design was a stratified, multistage 
area probability sample of households and persons.

  </div>
  <div class="project-image">
    <img src="/images/research/content/Picture1.png" alt="UMDES study">
  </div>
</div>

(2009). “The University of Michigan Dioxin Exposure Study: methods for an environmental exposure study of polychlorinated dioxins, furans and biphenyls”, *Environmental Health Perspectives*, **117**, 803-810.

<div class="project-block">
  <div class="project-text">

<p style="color: #2f6db5; font-weight: 700; font-size: 1.2rem; margin-bottom: 0.5rem;">
The 2013-2014 National Drug Abuse Treatment System Survey (NDATSS-2013)
</p>
provides management- and policy-relevant information about substance abuse treatment in 
the United States. Substance abuse treatment programs are chosen for the study by 
random selection from a list of all such programs stratified by program types, 
provided by the Substance Abuse and Mental Health Services Administration (SAMHSA). 
The data collection was done through interviews with program directors and clinical 
supervisors.

- D’Aunno, T., Pollack, H., **Chen, Q.**, and Friedmann, P. D. (2017). “Linkages between patient-centered medical homes and addiction treatment organizations: results from a national survey”, *Medical Care*, **55**(4): 379-383.
